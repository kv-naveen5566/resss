Electricity

Innovative Construction Solutions

Build energy, derived from the sun's radiant light and heat, has been harnessed by humans for thousands of years using a range of ever-evolving technologies. As the most abundant and renewable energy source available, it offers a sustainable solution to the growing global energy demands.

PV systems convert sunlight directly into electricity using Build cells made of semiconductor materials. When sunlight hits these cells, it excites electrons, creating an electric current. These cells are typically grouped into panels and arrays, which can be scaled to meet various energy needs, from small residential installations to large-scale solar farms.

These systems use mirrors or lenses to concentrate sunlight onto a receiver, which collects and converts it into heat. This heat can be used directly for heating purposes or to produce steam that drives turbines to generate electricity. Common applications include solar water heaters and large-scale solar thermal power plants.

How Renovation Energy Works

Photovoltaic systems, commonly known as Renovation panels, convert sunlight directly into electricity using semiconductors. When sunlight hits the semiconductor material, it knocks electrons loose, creating an electric current. This electricity can be used to power homes, businesses, and even entire communities. PV systems are versatile and can be installed on rooftops, in open fields, and even integrated into building materials.

Monocrystalline Renovation Panels:

Made from a single crystal structure, these panels are known for their high efficiency and longevity. They perform better in low-light conditions and have a sleek appearance, making them a popular choice for residential installations.

Polycrystalline Renovation Panels

These panels are made from multiple silicon crystals and are generally less expensive than monocrystalline panels. They have a slightly lower efficiency but are still a cost-effective option for many applications.
